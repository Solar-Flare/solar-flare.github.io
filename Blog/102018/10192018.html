Correlation/Causation

Date: 10/19/2018

About the pause between 10/2 to 10/19, this time period was kind of a terrible time period for me. Everyone really goes through one of those, though, and now I finally have time to do more brain dumps.

Anyways, there are three kinds of lies: Lies, damned lies, and statistics. The former two are self explanatory, but the third one is the interesting one.
What does it mean by statistics being a 'lie'?

If you go to this site, you will see a bunch of graphs that tend to follow very similar patterns. And according to statistics, that is showing correlation, as in, two sets of data are very closely similar or has a casual relationship; in other words the sets of data follow a similar pattern.

Many statistics makes it seem like correlation means one causes the other. And that's an implied bias - if two sets of data follow a similar pattern, one ought to cause another, right? In other words, causation.

Observe the first graph in the site above. It shows a set of data that represents US spending on science, space, and technology, and a set of data that represents suicides by hanging/strangulation/suffocation, and the data sets are very correlated; both go up at a similar rate.

But how does US spending on science/space/tech increase suicides by hanging/strangulation/suffocation? It just can't be explained (maybe if the increase was small we could attribute it to alien technology).

The reason for this is simply because an increase in population increases suicide count and also increases the GDP of the US - in other words, correlation doesn't imply causation.
