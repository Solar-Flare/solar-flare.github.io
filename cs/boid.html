Boid

Date: 07/11/2018

Literal Flying Pawns

My first ever pygame project!

There are these things called boids. Basically, they move in a direction that abides by three rules:
1. Separation: The boid steers away from nearby boids; it tries not to make a 'crowded' environment. In the image above, no two boids are on each other.
2. Alignment: The boid steers towards the average direction that nearby boids are going. In the image above, all boids are going in the same direction.
3. Cohesion: The boid steers towards the average position of nearby boids, to create like a lattice.


I also made it so that a new pawnboid is created when clicking on the screen.
Multiple errors rose throughout this challenge. This included:
1. Image rendering: Over time, the pawn images will literally defragment and explode. Turns out pygame is bad at turning images as for a 20x20 image it generates a larger and then burns it down to a 20x20. What happens over time is the loss of pixels and defragmentation. This was solved by constantly recreating the image and turning it once before the next recreation.
2. The initial initialization of the vector, position and acceleration positions were accidentally calculated in integers. This produced rather funny glitches like sudden warpings of the boids or static movement.
3. The boids will be all clumped together like moths to a lightbulb. Turns out the cohesion was too strong and changing that fixed that problem.
Overall, I got to reuse classes in python, utilize pygame and image rendering, which was pretty fun.
